This is handy to mirror a fake set of checkbox selection attributes to other inputs.
For instance, to manipulate a set of form inputs from outside the form.

Click any checkbox in the controller set. The related checkbox in the target set should mirror it's status.

To use:
  • Make sure you jQuery + the checkmate plugin
  • Make sure that your "fake" inputs and your "real" inputs have the same name attribute
  • Give your all of your "fake" inputs the same class. In this demo, I used "control".
  • Attach the checkmate() function to a clickhandler on your "fake" inputs:
    $('input.control').click(function(){ $(this).checkmate(); });
